## 1. Risks of Using a Laptop Without External Equipment
Working directly on a laptop can lead to:
- **Neck strain** due to looking downward at the screen  
- **Rounded shoulders** from leaning in  
- **Lower back pain** from unsupported seating  
- **Wrist strain or tendon issues** from typing on a low, cramped keyboard  
- **Eye strain** from improper screen distance  
- **General fatigue** from poor posture  

Most issues come from the fact that laptops force the **screen too low** and the **keyboard too close** to the body.

---

## 2. Ergonomic Equipment That Improves Posture
- **Laptop stand**: Raises the screen to eye level  
- **External keyboard and mouse**: Allows neutral arm and wrist posture  
- **Adjustable ergonomic chair**: Supports spine alignment  
- **Footrest** (if needed): Keeps hips and knees at 90 degrees  
- **External monitor**: Ideal for long sessions  
- **Desk with proper height** (elbows at a ~90-degree angle)  

---

## 3. Proper Workspace Setup Adjustments
- Screen top should be at or slightly below **eye level**  
- Screen should be about **an armâ€™s length** away  
- Chair height should allow **feet flat on the floor**  
- Hips and knees should be at **90 degrees**  
- Keyboard and mouse should keep arms relaxed and elbows near the body  
- Wrists should remain **neutral**, not bent upward

- Use the **20-20-20 rule** for eye strain (every 20 min, look 20 ft away for 20 sec)  
- Sit upright with shoulders relaxed  
- Stretch neck, wrists, and back throughout the day
