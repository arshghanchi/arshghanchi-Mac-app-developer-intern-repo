### 1. What Is Agile, and How Does It Differ From Traditional Project Management?
**Agile** is an iterative, flexible approach to software development focused on delivering value quickly and adapting to change.  
**Traditional project management (Waterfall)** works in fixed, sequential phases‚Äîrequirements ‚Üí design ‚Üí development ‚Üí testing ‚Üí delivery.

**Key differences:**

| Agile | Traditional (Waterfall) |
|-------|---------------------------|
| Iterative and incremental | Linear and sequential |
| Welcomes change | Resistant to change |
| Continuous feedback | Feedback mainly at end |
| Early and frequent delivery | Delivery only at final stage |
| Collaborative and adaptive | Structured and documentation-heavy |

Agile prioritises adaptability, customer value, and continuous improvement.

### 2. Core Values & Principles of Agile (Agile Manifesto)
**Values:**
- **Individuals and interactions** over processes and tools  
- **Working software** over comprehensive documentation  
- **Customer collaboration** over contract negotiation  
- **Responding to change** over following a fixed plan  

**Key principles that stand out include:**
- Deliver working software frequently  
- Welcome changing requirements  
- Maintain a sustainable pace  
- Close collaboration between developers and stakeholders  
- Continuous reflection and improvement  

---

### 3. Differences Between Scrum and Kanban
**Scrum**
- Works in **time-boxed sprints** (usually 1‚Äì2 weeks)  
- Roles include **Scrum Master, Product Owner, Development Team**  
- Ceremonies: sprint planning, sprint review, retrospective, daily standups  
- A backlog is planned into each sprint  
- More structured and ritual-based  

**Kanban**
- No fixed sprints **continuous flow**  
- Focuses on **visualising work**, limiting WIP (Work In Progress)  
- No strict roles or ceremonies required  
- Tasks move through the workflow at their own pace  
- Highly adaptable and great for variable workloads  

---

### 4. Why Focus Bear Leans Toward Kanban
Focus Bear prefers **Kanban** because:
- Work varies daily/weekly and doesn‚Äôt fit neatly into 2-week sprint boxes  
- Small team = simpler workflow with fewer compulsory meetings  
- Continuous improvement and fast response to changes suits the company‚Äôs style  
- Kanban reduces overhead and allows the team to stay flexible and focused

### 1. Biggest Benefits and Challenges of Agile

#### **Benefits**
- Faster delivery of features  
- Ability to pivot when requirements change  
- Better team collaboration
