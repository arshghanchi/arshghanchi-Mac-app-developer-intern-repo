## 1. Who Are Considered Vulnerable Populations?
Vulnerable populations in digital spaces may include:
- People with **ADHD**, **Autism**, Dyslexia, or other neurodivergences  
- Individuals with **executive dysfunction**  
- People with sensory sensitivities  
- Users who struggle with **motivation, focus, emotional regulation**, or **task initiation**  
- Those who rely heavily on digital tools for structure and day-to-day functioning  

**Challenges they may face include:**  
- Overwhelming UI/UX  
- Too many notifications  
- Poor navigation structure  
- Sensory overload  
- Confusing language  
- Information presented in long or unstructured chunks  

---

## 2. Ethical Considerations When Designing for Neurodivergent Individuals
- Keep UX **simple, predictable, and low-cognitive-load**  
- Avoid aggressive visuals or sounds that may cause sensory overwhelm  
- Provide **clear, direct communication** and consistent wording  
- Allow user autonomy and customization of notifications, reminders, and UI elements  
- Ensure the product does not shame, guilt, or pressure users  
- Validate user struggles and present supportive tone in messaging  

---

## 3. Making Interactions More Accessible for People with ADHD or Autism
- Use **simple, concise language**  
- Break tasks into small steps  
- Use clear headings and predictable layout  
- Reduce unnecessary animations or motion  
- Provide visual structure (icons, spacing, hierarchy)  
- Keep navigation intuitive and consistent  
- Offer flexible reminders and session lengths  
- Reinforce progress positively rather than punitively  

---

## 4. Supporting Neurodivergent Teammates
- Communicate clearly and directly  
- Avoid vague language or ambiguous instructions  
- Give written summaries of meetings or decisions  
- Respect differences in work rhythm and processing speed  
- Support asynchronous communication whenever possible  
- Provide early notice for meetings and deadlines  
- Encourage regular breaks and flexibility
