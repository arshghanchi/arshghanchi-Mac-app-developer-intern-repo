### 1. Main Agile Ceremonies & Their Purpose

#### **Daily Stand-ups**
- A short, focused meeting (usually 5‚Äì15 minutes).  
- Team members share:
  - What they worked on yesterday  
  - What they plan to work on today  
  - Any blockers  
- Keeps everyone aligned and aware of progress and dependencies.

---

#### **Sprint Planning (Scrum) vs. Continuous Prioritisation (Kanban)**

**Sprint Planning (Scrum)**  
- Happens at the start of each sprint (1‚Äì2 weeks).  
- Team commits to a set of tasks based on velocity.  
- Defines sprint goals and workload.

**Continuous Prioritisation (Kanban)**  
- No sprints, no fixed timeboxes.  
- PM continuously reprioritises tasks based on user needs and deadlines.  
- Developers pull tasks when ready, rather than committing to a batch.  
- More flexible and ideal for teams like Focus Bear.

---

#### **Retrospectives**
- A recurring ceremony (end of sprint or monthly in Kanban).  
- Team reflects on:
  - What went well  
  - What didn‚Äôt  
  - What can be improved  
- Helps improve workflow and collaboration continuously.

---

#### **Backlog Refinement**
- PM and developers clarify requirements, update user stories, and prioritise tasks.  
- Ensures upcoming tasks are well-defined, preventing confusion or delays.  
- In Kanban, refinement is done continuously rather than on a schedule.

---

### 2. How Agile Teams Collaborate Asynchronously & Across Time Zones
- Use Kanban boards for full visibility of task status  
- Share written updates instead of relying solely on meetings  
- Use tools like Slack/Discord, GitHub, documentation, Notion, Loom, etc.  
- Record meetings for people who cannot attend  
- Provide clear acceptance criteria and detailed notes  
- Tag teammates with actionable, informative messages  
- Keep communication structured and concise to avoid misunderstandings  

Async collaboration is essential in distributed teams like Focus Bear.
